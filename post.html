КОРЕЙСКИЕ МОДЕЛИ НАЦЕЛЕНЫ НА УКРАИНСКИЙ ФАШИОН
Visual artist/graphic novelist/designer/writer SUTU aka Sutu Eats Flies is one of the more intriguing artists that I've stumbled upon via Instagram. While the Australian artist's illustrations and creations are worthy of praise, it is his innovative use of futuristic technology and interactive applications that really allow him to stand out from the rest. If you visit his online store, you will see prints and graphic novels that involve an Augmented Reality (AR) and require the download of an app called Polaxis where you can place your phone over the art and see additional layers and 3D interaction. A picture of a man holding a ball of power, for example, can be presented over the phone and turned into an animated, moving image. Virtual reality in the form of a comic book. Welcome to 2016. In addition, according to his website, SUTU has also "created multimedia installations for the Australian National Museum and designed pilot training programs for the Australian Defence Force." I had the opportunity to speak with the artist while he was up in the northern heights of the globe and we talked about his forward-thinking ideas and what he has in store for the rest of the year. Get familiar with SUTU.
HEY, HOW'S IT GOING? Pretty good. HOW HAS 2016 BEEN TREATING YOU SO FAR? Off to a good start I guess. This is the time where I send out grant applications to a lot of Australian government bodies. I finished four off last week, including one for an animated series. Fingers crossed YOU LAST EMAIL SAID THAT YOU'RE IN SVALBARD AT THE MOMENT. CAN YOU ELABORATE ON WHAT YOU'RE DOING UP THERE? I’m actually on my way to do an artist residency in Finland but my wife and I always wanted to visit here so we took a detour for a week. Yesterday we drove snowmobiles across a glacier to an old Russian mining town. Today we visited the Seed Vault. In case you haven’t heard of it, it’s a place for storing seeds, millions of seeds from all around the world. Why? Well, just incase stupid humans get in a big war and destroy everything and then have to regrow. FYI the first ever withdrawal from the seed vault happened recently...the seeds were to regrow flora in Syria. HOW LONG HAVE YOU BEEN TAKING ILLUSTRATING SERIOUSLY/PROFESSIONALLY? Over ten years now. I started off doing graphic design, then I moved on to web/interactive design, then I did graphics for skateboards and snowboards and tees, etc. and then I started doing comics. And then I started combining my web skills with my comic skills. YOU SEEM TO BE ON THE FOREFRONT OF COMBINING COMICS WITH TECHNOLOGY. CAN YOU SPEAK ON HOW THIS CAME TO BE? Early on I started imagining entire story worlds for my standalone illustrations, so that’s what drove me to want to tell stories as comics. But at the same time that I was doing this my main job was designing interactive Flash websites. Adobe Flash was really popular at this time and it’s an easy tool for combining animation, graphics, and interactive programming, so it felt more natural for me try and tell a story this way, rather then just tell a story in a comic book format. My first main interactive comic was www.nawlz.com.
